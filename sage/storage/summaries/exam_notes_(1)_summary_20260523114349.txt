- The Scientific Method as a structured approach for knowledge acquisition in science

KEY CONCEPTS & DEFINITIONS:

Objective - Unbiased approach to knowledge acquisition.
Systematic Analysis – A methodical examination of phenomena or data sets, often using a structured framework like experiments or surveys.
Theory – An explanatory set that accounts for various observations and relationships within the research scope; it's an abstract representation derived from concrete experiences (observations).
Concept/Construct - General ideas used to categorize phenomena in qualitative studies, not directly measurable by numbers but essential for understanding.
Proposition – A statement proposing a relationship between concepts or constructs within the theory framework; testable through research methods.
Hypothesis – An initial assumption about variables that can be tested and potentially falsified during empirical investigation.
Level of Abstraction - Refers to how closely an idea is tied to specific, tangible instances (low) versus broad generalizations or principles applicable across situations (high).
